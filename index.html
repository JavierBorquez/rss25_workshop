Towards Reliable and Safe Autonomy

June 25, 2025

University of Southern California, Los Angeles

Robotics: Science and Systems

Safety is a fundamental challenge in deploying autonomous robotic systems, particularly as learning-based methods become central to decision-making and control. Ensuring reliability goes beyond achieving high accuracyâ€”it requires rigorous verification, well-defined safety metrics, and adherence to evolving regulatory standards. The growing complexity of autonomous systems demands a multidisciplinary approach to develop methods that ensure robustness, interpretability, and certifiability in real-world settings.

This workshop brings together researchers from machine learning, formal verification, robotics, and related fields to explore safety requirements and methodologies for achieving reliable autonomy. Discussions will focus on defining and measuring safety and developing techniques such as robustness against adversarial perturbations, verifiability of decision-making models, and interpretability in high-stakes environments. We will examine whether generalizable models alone are sufficient for safety, the trade-offs between interpretability and performance, and the role of formal methods in certifying autonomous systems.

In addition to academic advancements, the workshop will highlight industry-driven safety engineering tools, certification processes, and real-world applications. By fostering collaboration across disciplines, this workshop aims to bridge the gap between theory and practice, driving progress toward safe, trustworthy, and deployable autonomous systems.

Call for Papers

We invite researchers and practitioners to submit their papers to the workshop. Submissions should address topics related to safety in autonomous systems, including but not limited to:

  • Robustness against adversarial perturbations
  • Verifiability of decision-making models
  • Interpretability in high-stakes environments
  • Formal methods in certifying autonomous systems
